ABOUT

Palawan, an archipelagic province in the Philippines, is the country's largest province by area, situated in the Mimaropa region. Known as the Philippines' Last Frontier, its capital is Puerto Princesa. The islands stretch between Mindoro and Borneo, encompassing the South China and Sulu Seas.

Flora and Fauna

Palawan, a biodiverse island in the Philippines, is part of Sundaland and is home to numerous endemic species. With over 700,000 hectares of forests, it is considered the country's "last biodiversity frontier." The island has Biosphere Reserve status and is recognized for its beautiful natural seascapes and landscapes. Palawan's coral reefs cover 35% of the nation's total, while its beaches have been voted the best in Asia. Notable discoveries on the island include the shrew-eating pitcher plant and the purple crab.
