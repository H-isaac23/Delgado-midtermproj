EL NIDO

Hover over an image.

Small Lagoon, a charming destination in Miniloc Island, offers beauty in a more intimate setting. Accessible by swimming or kayaking through a crack in a limestone wall, visitors can enjoy the serene turquoise waters surrounded by cliffs. The natural pool provides a peaceful spot for floating or kayaking.

El Nido's Secret Beach, accessible only by swimming through a hole in limestone cliffs, inspired Alex Garland's novel "The Beach" and its film adaptation starring Leonardo DiCaprio. Beware of strong waves; tour guides provide life vests and assistance for this challenging yet rewarding experience.

CORON

Hover over an image.

Kayangan Lake, reached via a 15-minute hill trek, offers stunning views of blue waters and limestone cliffs. Known as one of the cleanest lakes in the Philippines, it's maintained by the Tagbanua tribe. Visit early to avoid crowds and enjoy swimming in the pristine waters.

Twin Lagoon offers two paradise spots separated by a karst wall. The first lagoon gets crowded, while the second is accessed by ladder or swimming depending on the tide. Experience a unique blend of warm and cold water while swimming in the lagoons!

PUERTO PRINCESA

Hover over an image.

Honda Bay in Puerto Princesa offers island-hopping adventures, showcasing white sand beaches and marine-rich islands. Though often overshadowed by El Nido and Coron, it remains a top tourist destination in the Philippines.

Firefly watching in Iwahig and Irawan Rivers is a unique Puerto Princesa experience. Board paddle boats and cruise through mangrove forests, witnessing thousands of fireflies, glowing plankton, and constellations for a serene escape from city life.

DELICACIES

Hover over an image.

Tamilok, a mollusk harvested from mangrove trees, is a popular exotic dish in Palawan. Often served in Puerto Princesa restaurants, its taste resembles oysters.

Crocodile Sisig, a high-protein, low-fat dish, is another Palawan specialty. With a taste similar to chicken, it's available at Ka Inato Restaurant on Rizal Avenue.

Danggit Lamayo, sun-dried marinated Rabbitfish, is a popular Coron dish served for breakfast at hotels and guesthouses.

Chao Long noodles, introduced by Vietnamese immigrants, are flat rice noodles in a sweet-savory broth. Try them at Rene's Saigon Restaurant on Rizal Avenue.
